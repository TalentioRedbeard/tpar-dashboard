## Clip 2 — Clocking in + your status buttons (1:17, v3)

**2.1 — the clock**
> Your day starts with one tap. Clock in on My Day when you begin, clock out when you're done — that's the whole job. If you forget, the app will nudge you; if you're at a job site, it'll offer to do it for you.

**2.2 — the status bar, on the job**
> Every job carries this status bar — the whole visit, step by step. Scheduling's handled for you; your presses start here: On My Way when you roll. Start when you're in it. Presentation, then the work. Collect when it's paid, and Finish closes it out. Tap each one as it happens — no paperwork, no calls.

**2.3 — why it matters**
> Here's what those taps do: dispatch sees where things stand, the schedule stays true, and the customer gets told what's happening — without anybody chasing you down or calling you mid-job. One press covers everyone.

**2.4 — the GPS assist + close**
> And the app watches your back: pull up to a job, and it offers the press for you — just tap yes. Miss one? Fix it right there on the job page. The bar, pressed as it happens. That's the whole system.

## Clip 4 — Building an estimate + Price it with me (2:32)

**4.1 — the builder**
> This is where estimates get built — options for the customer, never pressure. Every line is the same math: hours, crew, materials, and your judgment on the modifiers. Type, category, work type, item — then the numbers. That's the TPAR way: time, material, judgment.

**4.2 — describe the work**
> But when you're standing in a yard not sure what to charge — this is your move. Price it with me. Describe the work in your own words, like you'd tell another plumber. What you saw, what you think it needs, what's weird about the house.

**4.3 — while it reads (over the real wait)**
> While it reads, remember the ladder: nothing opens without something in writing. A diagnostic fee is about an hour of our rate — its job is findings and a path forward, not a fix. And there's no flat price for judgment work — never has been. The price comes from the time, the materials, and the answers only you can give.

**4.4 — the questions**
> And here they come — the judgment questions. What system, what access, what are we actually testing. These are the modifiers. Nobody knows these answers but the tech on site — that's why it asks you.

**4.5 — while it builds (over the real wait)**
> Now it builds. And here's the rule that protects everybody: the machine never invents a price. It proposes the hours, the crew, the materials — and the math is computed from our rate ladder, same as if you'd filled the form by hand. If it can't find the work in our pricebook, it says so honestly, and the custom item goes to Danny's review queue.

**4.6 — the lines land + close**
> There they are — priced, grounded, and yours to edit. Add them to the option, then read every number like you wrote it, because now you did. You're the last set of eyes before a customer sees this. Options, not pressure — and never send what you haven't read.

## Clip 6 — Receipts + photos (1:09)

**6.1 — the rule**
> Receipts have one rule: snap it the minute it's in your hand. Supply house counter, gas pump, wherever — Receipt tile, photo, done. Don't let it ride in the truck, because truck receipts die in trucks.

**6.2 — what happens to it**
> That photo isn't going into a shoebox. It lands on the job, the cost tracks itself, and the office stops re-typing crumpled paper. Every dollar we spend finds its job — which is how we know what work actually pays.

**6.3 — photos + close**
> Same instinct with job photos: before and after, and always before you haul the old one off. Photos go in the app — on the job — not in a text to the office, where they get buried. If it happened on the job, it lives on the job. That's the habit.
