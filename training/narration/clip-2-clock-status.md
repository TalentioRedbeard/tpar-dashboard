# Clip 2 — "Clocking in + your status buttons" (~85 seconds)

The rollout centerpiece: the lifecycle habit. Storyboard: /me clock section → a job page
with the status buttons (SPOTLIGHT ONLY — never press; they fire real triggers) → the
geofence/clock-suggestion area on /me → close. Beats sync to the manifest.

**Beat 1 — the clock, on My Day**
> Your day starts with one tap. Clock in on My Day when you begin, clock out when you're done — that's the whole job. If you forget, the app will nudge you; if you're at a job site, it'll offer to do it for you.

**Beat 2 — the status buttons, on the job**
> Every job has four buttons, and you press each one as it happens: On My Way when you roll. Start when you're in it. Finish when the work's done. Collect when it's paid. That's it — no paperwork, no calls.

**Beat 3 — why it matters**
> Here's what those taps do: dispatch sees where things stand, the schedule stays true, and the customer gets told what's happening — without anybody chasing you down or calling you mid-job. One press covers everyone.

**Beat 4 — the GPS assist + close**
> And the app watches your back: pull up to a job, and it offers the press for you — just tap yes. Miss one? Fix it right there on the job page. Four buttons, pressed when they happen. That's the whole system.
