**Beat 1 — title card (host slot)**
> Welcome to the TPAR app. This is your toolbox for everything that isn't a wrench — built by us, around how we actually work. In the next few minutes, you'll have a basic outline of this app and how to use it. Each following clip will give a more thorough tutorial for each given page, what it does, and how to use it to assist you in your day.

**Beat 2 — My Day**
> Every day should start on the My Day page. My Day is your home base for navigating your day as a whole. Your clock-in, clock-out button, the day's expectations, the team whiteboard, your quick actions, today's appointments, and your daily wrap. One page, with the intent to host your daily work functions.

**Beat 3 — Jobs / Find**
> Every job and every customer we've ever served is accessible from this page, scoped to any given technician's schedule. Search by name, by address — or just describe what you remember about the job. This page lists and helps you dynamically search for any given job. The app holds everything the company has ever done — what you see is scoped to the work you're assigned, past and present. As it should be.

**Beat 4 — Estimates + Price it with me**
> The Estimates page — navigate to any given estimate, or start building a new one. Create options for the customer: informative, never pressuring. When you don't know what to charge, or you're not sure what line items to use? Use Price it with me and describe the work in your own words: it asks you the judgment questions, then builds the line item — or line items — with you, priced the TPAR way. Time, material, and your judgment.

**Beat 5 — Comms**
> Comms — every text and every phone call between us and a customer, in one thread of record. Before you walk into a job cold, catch up here on what's already been said — scoped, like everything, to your own work.

**Beat 6 — Gallery**
> Gallery — every job photo we've ever taken, searchable. What you shoot on a job lands here and stays findable — for you, for the office, for the next tech out there.

**Beat 7 — Shopping**
> Shopping — parts and suppliers: the supplier directory, and a place to log what you need the moment you need it. It's backed by real prices from our own receipts — so what a part actually costs is never a guess.

**Beat 8 — Settings**
> Settings — make the app fit you: your reminders, simple mode, how you like your information. The app bends to you — not the other way around.

**Beat 9 — Ask + push to Danny**
> The Ask function is a feature you will find on every page in the app. Stuck on anything? Pricing, process, where something lives — or have a question about the data displayed on the very page you're on? Ask. It knows how we work and it knows our data. Ask the question, give it thirty seconds, and get an answer. Unsatisfied with the answer, or question its accuracy? Push the question directly to Danny.

**Beat 10 — How-to / Field Guide + close**
> Last stop: How-to — the manual that's actually short. What every button does — and the Field Guide, which holds how we carry ourselves: the principles, the requirement of getting paid, and exactly what to do or who to call when you're stuck. That's the map. Next clip: a tutorial on the My Day page, up close.
