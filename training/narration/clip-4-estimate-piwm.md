# Clip 4 — "Building an estimate + Price it with me" (~2:45 — the deep dive)

The waits are REAL and left in on purpose — the narration teaches the money ladder while
the machine thinks, which is exactly the pacing a tech will live. Storyboard:
/estimate/new?customer=<Marketing Test> → builder overview → open Price it with me →
type the description → Start (extract runs ~25s, narrated) → answer 2 questions →
Build my line items (~50s, narrated) → proposed cards → Add to Option 1 → close.
GUARDRAILS: never submit/push the estimate; adding lines to the option is client-side only.

**Beat 1 — the builder**
> This is where estimates get built. Options for the customer, never pressure. Every line is the same math: hours, crew, materials, and your judgment on the modifiers. Type, category, work type, item. Then the numbers. That's the TPAR way: time, material, judgment.

**Beat 2 — describe the work**
> But when you're standing in a yard not sure what to charge, this is your move. Price it with me. Describe the work in your own words, like you'd tell another plumber. What you saw, what you think it needs, what's weird about the house.

**Beat 3 — while it reads (the extract wait)**
> While it reads, remember the ladder: nothing opens without something in writing. A diagnostic fee is about an hour of our rate. Its job is findings and a path forward, not a fix. And there's no flat price for judgment work. Never has been. The price comes from the time, the materials, and the answers only you can give.

**Beat 4 — the questions**
> And here they come. The judgment questions. What system, what access, what are we actually testing. These are the modifiers. Nobody knows these answers but the tech on site. That's why it asks you.

**Beat 5 — while it builds (the build wait)**
> Now it builds. And here's the rule that protects everybody: the machine never invents a price. It proposes the hours, the crew, the materials. And the math is computed from our rate ladder, same as if you'd filled the form by hand. If it can't find the work in our pricebook, it says so honestly, and the custom item goes to Danny's review queue.

**Beat 6 — the lines land + close**
> There they are. Priced, grounded, and yours to edit. Add them to the option, then read every number like you wrote it, because now you did. You're the last set of eyes before a customer sees this. Options, not pressure. And never send what you haven't read.
